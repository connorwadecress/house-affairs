# Community Strategy

## Baseline Community Path

1. Publish consistent DJ sessions.
2. Make every session easy to share.
3. Invite comments, track feedback, and respond.
4. Build a lightweight owned audience channel, such as a website waitlist/email list or WhatsApp, after platform access, privacy handling, and ownership are confirmed.
5. Invite a small trusted group to recordings.
6. Use audience signal to support venue nights and ticketed events.

## Current Public Engagement Signals

Source: [[04 - Content & Media/YouTube Snapshot - 2026-07-01]]

- Viewers are already commenting with encouragement, jokes, timestamps, and direct production feedback.
- A viewer contributed timestamped track cues, which suggests community members may help enrich sessions if prompted.
- At least one listener used a session while running, which suggests House Affairs can serve more moments than only nightlife.
- Audio quality feedback was acknowledged publicly by the House Affairs account, which is a good pattern to continue.

## Community Principles

- Music-first.
- Pretoria-rooted.
- Inclusive without becoming generic.
- Consistent enough to build trust.
- Small and credible before large and diluted.

## Open Questions

- Who moderates community channels?
- What is the minimum privacy and consent standard for filming attendees?
- What should the first waitlist capture: event updates, booking intent, collaboration interest, or all of these as explicit categories?
