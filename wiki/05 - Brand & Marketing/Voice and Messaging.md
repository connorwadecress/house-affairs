# Voice and Messaging

## Internal Voice

Direct, ambitious, music-first, Pretoria-rooted, and comfortable with advanced technology and agentic operations.

## Public Voice

Clear, confident, welcoming, and credible. The public voice should make people want to listen, attend, collaborate, or book without feeling like the brand is gatekeeping.

## Core Messages

- House music has a home in Pretoria.
- Modern house music. Real connections.
- Sessions first, community always, events next.
- Get your affairs in order.
- Join the movement.
- Carefully selected sounds that move people.

## Words and Phrases To Use Carefully

- "Collective" - good, but define whether it means performers, organisers, or community.
- "Venue" - long-term vision only, not current reality.
- "Registered business" - do not publish details until verified.
- "Weekly" - use only if cadence is current and provable.
- "Movement" - useful and already present on YouTube, but should be backed by growing community proof over time.

## Raw Language Handling

Some raw founding wording is preserved in [[00 - Meta/Founding Story - Verbatim|Founding Story - Verbatim]] for internal source accuracy. Public copy should translate frustration with mainstream nightlife into positive positioning, such as:

- "Pretoria needs more intentional house music spaces."
- "A home for modern house music and real connection."
- "Building the house music movement Pretoria has been missing."
