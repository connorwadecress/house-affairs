## First Research Findings - 2026-07-01

These findings are source-backed planning notes, not legal advice.

- **SAMRO:** Official SAMRO user/licensing pages say music users who play music in the course of business need a music usage licence, including promoters, pubs, mobile DJs, clubs, restaurants, shopping malls, and live music venues. SAMRO notes that it administers performing rights.
- **SAMPRA:** Official SAMPRA FAQ says any person, entity, or venue that plays or broadcasts sound recordings/music in public must get a SAMPRA licence. It also states that a SAMRO licence and SAMPRA licence cover different rights, and that a mobile DJ licence does not cover venue premises use.
- **YouTube:** Official YouTube sources say Content ID can block, monetize, or track videos, and actions may vary by geography. A Content ID claim is different from a copyright removal request/strike, but uploaders should not dispute claims unless they have the necessary rights.
- **City of Tshwane:** City event compliance content says event organisers/owners should present events to the Events Joint Operations Committee three weeks before their event, submit the JOC application form to eventsjoc@tshwane.gov.za, and comply with safety and by-law requirements. The event licence page lists requirements including a compulsory by-law workshop, ID copy, application form, and food-handler health certificate where applicable.

## Practical Rule For House Affairs

Before any public, paid, ticketed, filmed, or venue-branded event, House Affairs must identify:

- Whether the venue already has relevant SAMRO/SAMPRA coverage.
- Whether the DJ/promoter needs separate SAMRO/SAMPRA licensing.
- Whether the event is venue-hosted, House Affairs-hosted, ticketed, private, or public.
- Whether Tshwane JOC/event licence processes apply.
- Whether filming consent/releases are required.
- Who carries liability, security, door policy, refunds, and insurance.
