# Pretoria House Music Market

## Research Question

Is there an underserved audience for house and electronic music experiences in Pretoria, and what evidence supports it?

## Current Short Answer

Evidence supports Pretoria as a credible house/electronic music market, but not yet a proven House Affairs commercial opportunity. The strongest support is public cultural evidence from Boiler Room, WHYLY's Pretoria/Gauteng club-night positioning, historical House 22 deep-house references, and current venue/event infrastructure around Pretoria hospitality and live-music spaces. The missing proof is direct venue appetite, House Affairs audience conversion, and event economics.

## Findings

1. Pretoria has credible house-music heritage and public external recognition. Boiler Room called Pretoria a significant house-music city and hosted Pretoria-focused programming with house/electronic artists.
2. WHYLY is the strongest current Pretoria-specific scene reference found in this sprint. Public event evidence positions it as a dance-music club night with residents, ticketing, venue movement, and 18+ rules.
3. The founder-mentioned venues are plausible but not yet proven as House Affairs venues. Moka fits filmed/community sessions better than a confirmed DJ night; Lucky Rodrigo and Billy Boys need direct event/music appetite checks.
4. The broader Gauteng scene shows repeatable mechanics: recurring series, residents plus guests, ticketing, strict door/safety rules, venue-specific identity, and content/community extensions.
5. Compliance is not optional. SAMRO, SAMPRA, City of Tshwane, and YouTube sources all create specific verification work before paid public events or public DJ uploads are treated as operationally ready.

## Research Needed

- Direct venue conversations for Moka, Lucky Rodrigo, Billy Boys, Railways Cafe, and any other shortlist candidate.
- Current status and operating details for House 22.
- WHYLY deeper teardown: venue history, cadence, residents, ticket pricing, artwork, and audience mechanics.
- Audience size indicators from House Affairs private analytics, venue attendance estimates, ticket scans, and social platform analytics.
- University/student nightlife patterns and transport/safety realities.
- Compliance confirmation for SAMRO, SAMPRA, Tshwane JOC/event licence, venue licence coverage, filming releases, insurance, and ticket terms.

## Limits

This note does not prove House Affairs demand, ticket sales, attendance, profitability, or venue willingness. Public listings prove that comparable events are marketed; they do not prove that House Affairs can replicate them.

## Recommendation

Use a two-step validation path:

1. **Desktop-to-call validation:** prepare a venue introduction and call two prospects only after internal approval.
2. **Small proof event:** start with a non-ticketed or venue-hosted low-risk DJ slot before attempting a House Affairs-branded ticketed night.
