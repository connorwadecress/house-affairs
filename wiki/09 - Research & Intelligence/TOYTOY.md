# TOYTOY

## Question

What can House Affairs learn from TOYTOY as a Johannesburg underground residency model?

## Short Answer

TOYTOY shows the power of a consistent weekly residency, residents plus guest artists, strict dancefloor culture, and clear event rules. It is a reference model, not a direct venue prospect.

## Confirmed Facts

- TOYTOY's Bandcamp page states it is a weekly Friday night residency at And Club and TOYTOY Music, based in Johannesburg, South Africa.
- Resident Advisor event pages list TOYTOY events at And Club, 39 Gwi Gwi Mrwebi St, Johannesburg, with techno/house genres, residents and guests, 18+ minimum age, and ticket costs.
- RA event copy records an And Club no-photo/no-video policy on the dance floor and garden, plus "rave safe" and street parking guidance.

## Mechanics To Learn

- Weekly cadence can create habit and identity.
- Residents give a night continuity; guests add novelty.
- A no-photo/no-video policy is a deliberate culture choice that can make the night feel more present and underground.
- Practical rules such as ID, parking, and closed shoes reduce ambiguity at the door.

## What House Affairs Should Not Copy Blindly

- A no-photo policy conflicts with House Affairs' current content-first strategy unless separated into event-only contexts.
- Weekly public events are likely too operationally heavy before House Affairs has audience proof and venue relationships.

## Recommendation

Borrow the resident-plus-guest structure and clear culture rules. Do not borrow the weekly cadence until House Affairs has repeatable operations and a venue partner.
