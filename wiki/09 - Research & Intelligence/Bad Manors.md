# Bad Manors

## Question

What can House Affairs learn from Bad Manors as a smaller music-bar/social-space reference?

## Short Answer

Bad Manors is useful because it sits between a restaurant/bar and a club. Public evidence frames it as a food and music bar with flexible genre programming, fresh-DJ pathways, and a social-space identity.

## Confirmed Facts

- InYourPocket lists Bad Manors at 39 Gwi Gwi Mrwebi, Carfax, Newtown, Johannesburg and describes it as a food and music bar.
- InYourPocket says its music includes speed garage, house, Afro-tech, and more, and references Detention Radio's Night School for fresh DJs.
- Quicket evidence shows Bad Manors hosting a hard/dark/industrial techno event on 30 May 2026 with an underground/intimate framing.
- Resident Advisor evidence shows additional genre flexibility through a drum-and-bass event at Bad Manors in January 2026.

## Mechanics To Learn

- A smaller venue can be a music-bar/social-space hybrid, not only a nightclub.
- Open-decks or fresh-DJ programming can build community and performer pipeline.
- A venue can host multiple genre lanes if each night is clearly framed.
- Food and drinks can make the venue feel social before the dancefloor peaks.

## What House Affairs Should Not Copy Blindly

- Bad Manors' genre spread may be wider than House Affairs' house-music identity.
- House Affairs should not dilute its sound just to fill a bar calendar.

Use Bad Manors as the closest reference for a future House Affairs "music bar" relationship: a small recurring night, clear host identity, rotating DJs, and community-friendly atmosphere.
