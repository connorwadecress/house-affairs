# Website Plan

## Current Known State

- Founder statement says House Affairs owns `house-affairs.org`.
- Domain ownership and DNS access are not yet verified in this repository.
- Connor has now requested planning for a public brochure-style website with owned waitlist functionality.
- Connor named Resend for waitlist email delivery, Vercel as a candidate hosting option, Google Search Console for search visibility, and PostHog as a possible analytics option.
- No website code has been created yet.

## First Website Hypothesis

The first site should be simple but visually strong:

- Brand statement.
- Latest session embed/link.
- DJ roster.
- Social links.
- Upcoming events or "coming soon" only when true.
- Contact or booking inquiry path.
- Owned waitlist/signup form after privacy handling, storage, and email delivery are defined.

The current detailed planning note is [[Public Website and Waitlist Blueprint]].

## Product Intent

The site should help House Affairs:

- Look credible and current to listeners, venues, collaborators, artists, and potential event partners.
- Capture owned demand signal instead of relying only on social platforms.
- Give people a way to stay in contact, hear about future events, book House Affairs, or collaborate.
- Support SEO and public discovery for the House Affairs name, sessions, DJs, and future events.

## Do Not Build Until Verified

- Domain access.
- Official links.
- Brand assets.
- Content links.
- Contact destination.
- Hosting choice.
- Database/storage provider for waitlist data.
- Privacy, consent, unsubscribe, and retention requirements.
- Resend account, verified sender domain, and production email configuration.
- Analytics choice and consent/privacy handling.
