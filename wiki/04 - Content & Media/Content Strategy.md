# Content Strategy

## Current Content Wedge

Weekly hour-long DJ sessions and recorded mixes are the starting engine for House Affairs.

## Content Pillars

- **Sessions:** Full DJ sets from House Affairs DJs and guests.
- **Locations:** Rooftops, sunsets, koppies, hills, and scenic Pretoria locations.
- **Community:** Real people, friends, and eventually audience-attended recordings.
- **Music identity:** Modern house music and electronic music.
- **Behind the scenes:** Setup, location scouting, DJ prep, and post-session moments.

## Baseline Publishing Model

- Long-form set on YouTube.
- Short-form clips and session artwork on Instagram.
- Session recap and call-to-action after publishing.
- Track performance metrics after each release.
- Capture public YouTube comments, requests, and feedback after each release using [[YouTube Capture Workflow]].

## First YouTube Learning Snapshot

Source: [[YouTube Snapshot - 2026-07-01]]

- Session 002 had the highest public views in the first snapshot: 188 views.
- Session 001 had the highest public likes in the first snapshot: 19 likes.
- Public feedback is mainly positive and socially supportive.
- The first concrete production request is audio quality: louder, less muffled, and clearly stereo.
- Track timestamps/chapters are valuable because a viewer contributed timestamped cues for Session 001.

## Do Not Claim Yet

- Consistent weekly cadence unless current publish dates prove it.
- Audience size beyond verified platform metrics.
- Official partnerships.
- Monetization status.
