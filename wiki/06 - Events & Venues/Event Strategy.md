# Event Strategy

## Strategic Progression

1. Recorded sets with no public attendance.
2. Small invited audience recordings.
3. House Affairs nights at existing venues.
4. Recurring ticketed events.
5. Long-term dedicated House Affairs venue or space.

## Minimum Viable Event Hypothesis

A small, curated House Affairs night at a Pretoria venue can validate whether the content audience converts into real attendance.

## First Research Implications - 2026-07-01

The first Pretoria-first venue and scene research sprint points to a lower-risk path:

1. Treat Moka Social Club as a possible filmed-session prospect first, not a confirmed party venue.
2. Treat Lucky Rodrigo and Billy Boys & Co as possible hospitality DJ-night prospects, pending direct confirmation of music fit, booking process, sound setup, filming permission, and economics.
3. Use WHYLY, Truth JHB, TOYTOY, Bad Manors, Sandton House Club, Fresh House Flava, and Boiler Room Pretoria as reference models for programming, promotion, venue fit, and compliance questions.
4. Do not run ticketed or public audience events until the team has a written compliance split covering venue responsibilities, organizer responsibilities, music rights, event permissions, filming consent, safety, and ticketing.

See [[09 - Research & Intelligence/Pretoria Venue and Scene Research Sprint - 2026-07-01|Pretoria Venue and Scene Research Sprint - 2026-07-01]] and [[06 - Events & Venues/Venue Pipeline|Venue Pipeline]].

## Event Success Criteria

- Safe and well-run.
- Music direction is clear.
- Venue relationship improves.
- Audience feedback is captured.
- Costs and revenue are recorded.
- Content output strengthens the brand.

## Risks

- Venue mismatch.
- Poor turnout.
- Unclear revenue split.
- Music rights/licensing issues.
- Sound/noise restrictions.
- Safety and transport.
- Inadequate filming consent.

## Required Before Ticketed Events

- Written decision from the team.
- Venue agreement or written confirmation.
- Budget and break-even model.
- Compliance check, including SAMRO/SAMPRA/music-rights responsibilities, municipal event requirements if applicable, filming consent, insurance/safety split, and ticketing terms.
- Promotion plan.
- Owner for event operations.
- Post-event reporting template.
