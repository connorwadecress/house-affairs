# Operating Blueprint

House Affairs uses this repository as an agentic wiki and operating memory.

This scaffold is adapted from the provided `Agentic Wiki Blueprint.md` and `Two-Founder Project Blueprint.md`, with one adjustment: House Affairs is a founding collective rather than a strict two-founder project. The same principle still applies: preserve distinct voices, record who decided what, and do not manufacture consensus.

## Core Rule

The wiki is the source of operational truth.

Agents and humans must:

1. Read before acting.
2. Separate evidence from assumptions.
3. Record decisions, correspondence, research, and status changes.
4. Leave the project easier to resume than they found it.

## Evidence Labels

- **Confirmed:** proven by founder statement, source note, screenshot, contract, official record, code, log, test, or verified platform record.
- **Assumption:** plausible but unproven.
- **Open question:** unknown and decision-relevant.
- **Founder/team view:** one person's stated view, not automatically collective consensus.
- **Joint decision:** explicitly agreed, delegated, or accepted through the working agreement.
- **Rejected:** considered and intentionally not chosen.

## Default Wiki Flow

1. Raw source goes into [[Founding Story - Verbatim]], [[Correspondence Log]], or [[Source Materials]].
2. Interpretation goes into the relevant strategy, business, content, or operations note.
3. Decisions go into [[Joint Decisions Log]].
4. Unknowns go into [[Open Questions]].
5. Tasks go into [[07 - Operations/Task Board|Task Board]].

## Do Not Invent

- Business registration numbers.
- Domain registrar details.
- Platform credentials.
- Venue agreements.
- Event permits.
- Revenue, costs, budgets, or ticket prices.
- Music licensing status.
- Audience demographics.
- Technical stack versions.
- Test results.

## Quality Bar

A new agent should be able to read [[Home|Home]], source materials, and relevant notes, then continue without relying on private chat history.
